Complex Euclidean spaces.

Let \(\Sigma\) be an alphabet, then the set of all functions from \(\Sigma\) to the complex numbers \(\C\), denoted \(\C^{\Sigma}\), forms a vector space of dimension \(|\Sigma|\) with

  1. Addition: \((u+v)(a) = u(a)+v(a) \ \forall a \in \Sigma\).

  2. Scalar multiplication: \((\alpha u)(a) = \alpha u(a) \ \forall \alpha \in \C \ \forall a \in \Sigma\).

  • Inner product is defined as \[\< u,v \> = \sum_{a \in \Sigma} \overline{u(a)} v(a)\]

  • Euclidean norm of vectors is defined as \[\Vert u \Vert = \sqrt{\<u,u\>} = \left(\sum_{a \in \Sigma} |u(a)|^2 \right)^{\frac{1}{2}}\]

  • \(p\)-norm of vectors is defined as \[\Vert u \Vert_p = \left(\sum_{a \in \Sigma} |u(a)|^p \right)^{\frac{1}{p}}\]

  • Direct sum of complex Euclidean spaces \[\spc X_1 \oplus \cdots \oplus \spc X_n = \C^{\Sigma_1 \cup \cdots \cup \Sigma_n}\]

  • Tensor product of complex Euclidean spaces \[\spc X_1 \otimes \cdots \otimes \spc X_n = \C^{\Sigma_1 \times \cdots \times \Sigma_n}\]
